Every American is entitled to adequate physical affection.

The DPA enforces federal standards for hugs, hand-holding, shoulder pats, head-pets, and other forms of warm physical contact. If a friend, partner, family member, or coworker has failed to meet their statutory affection obligations, you have the right to file a formal complaint.

Our Mission

Protecting the right to be held, hugged, and gently patted on the back.

The Department of Physical Affection is the principal federal agency responsible for the regulation, enforcement, and equitable distribution of physical affection across the United States. We work with citizens, family units, and workplace HR departments to ensure no American goes more than seventy-two (72) consecutive hours without meaningful physical contact, in accordance with 42 U.S.C. § 1983(b)(hug).

I

Investigate

Our field officers review every complaint and conduct on-site warmth assessments where appropriate.

II

Mediate

Trained Affection Mediators help resolve disputes between parties through structured embraces and forehead-kiss arbitration.

III

Enforce

When informal resolution fails, the DPA may issue a Cuddle Compliance Order under section 7(b) of the Affection Standards Act.

Your Rights

Under federal law, you are entitled to:

One (1) Hug Per Day, Minimum

Of no less than four (4) seconds in duration, with both arms engaged. Side-hugs do not satisfy this requirement except in workplace settings.

Reasonable Hand-Holding

While walking, watching films, or experiencing emotional distress. Sweaty palms are not grounds for refusal.

Head-Pat Access

On request, and without commentary on hair condition. Aggressive ruffling is not a substitute for a gentle pat.

Shoulder Squeeze on Demand

During moments of stress, achievement, or low-grade existential dread. Must be firm but not therapeutic in nature.

Forehead Kiss (Conditional)

Available to citizens in established relationships of 90+ days. See Form DPA-302 for exemptions.

Right to Cuddle

During movies, thunderstorms, and hospital visits. Cannot be unilaterally revoked without 48 hours' written notice.

Reportable Violations

Common forms of affection neglect.

If any of the following describe your situation, you may have grounds to file a complaint:

  • Failure to hug upon arrival or departure
  • "I'm not really a hugger" defense (invalid under § 4.2)
  • Pats on the back substituted for genuine embrace
  • Refusal to hold hands in public
  • Premature termination of cuddle (under 6 minutes)
  • Side-hug fraud during sentimental occasions
  • Cold shoulder issued without 24-hour notice
  • Failure to cuddle during thunderstorms
  • Limp, non-committal hug (the "wet noodle")
  • Refusal to share blanket on couch
  • Patting the head of a pet but not the citizen
  • Withholding affection as leverage

Are you experiencing an affection shortfall?

Filing a complaint is free, confidential, and takes less than three (3) minutes. A DPA Field Officer will review your case within one business day.
